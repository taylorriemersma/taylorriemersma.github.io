NCAA Bracket Post-Mortem

2021-04-09

Overall, my bracket did pretty well. This is the best performing bracket that I have ever had and plan to bust out the code next year as well. I ended up in the 99.7 percentile on ESPN’s tournament challenge, ultimately placing just above 40,000. While I didn’t win the nation, I did claim victory in the class by 10 points. All it takes is one first round matchup I now know.

I missed eight games total in the first round. They are:

Ohio-Virginia (Virginia had a 72.8% chance to win.) LSU-St. Bonaventure (LSU was supposed to lose.) BYU-UCLA (BYU had a 90.8% chance to win.) Texas-Abiline Christian (I didn’t see this coming and neither did you.) Purdue-North Texas (Ditto.) Ohio State-Oral Roberts (Ditto.) Tennessee-Oregon State (This was a tossup in the model. 51.6% Tenn to 28.4% OSU.) San Diego State-Syracuse (SDSU was supposed to win with a prediction of 64.6%.)

Some of the upsets may have been predictable, but others not so much. I would have never guessed UCLA would be in the Final Four. In my individual bracket, I did have Syracuse winning, but that was just a gut feeling. There was little to no logic behind that pick.

I believe I did a little bit of overfitting. I’m not sure what I would do in the future, but I would not go 100% by the bracket. I had four factors overall and maybe I need to only do two. OR maybe I need more. Only time will tell. Trial and error.

I know for certain that I would not have done as well without the model. I created another bracket to do on my own without the help of the code and rather than being in 40,000, I finished 8 Millionth.
